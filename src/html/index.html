How much does the value of real estate in Dubai increase?

Dubai is fast becoming an international trade and tourist hub. It is one of the safest cities in the world with one of the fastest growing economies in the region. The average annual return on investment in Dubai ranges from 5% to 8.4%.

In May 2022, 6,652 sale transactions were registered in the Dubai real estate market, which is 51.60% more than in May 2021, which is the highest figure for May in the last 10 years.

The first quarter of 2022 saw the largest number of real estate transactions recorded in a single quarter since 2010, with 25,972 transactions.

Buying a property in Dubai is relatively easy, whether you want the perfect home to live in or a profitable investment. The process of buying property in Dubai involves four main legal steps. To answer the question of how to buy property in Dubai, we will analyze the legal aspects in chronological order.

What is the process of buying property in Dubai?

1. CONCLUSION OF THE AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE BUYER AND THE SELLER

When it comes to buying property in Dubai and the UAE, you have two options: pay cash or apply for a mortgage. The ability to negotiate is perhaps the biggest benefit you get when you buy a property with cash instead of a mortgage. Cash buyers have a better idea of their budget and can therefore bargain at a lower price.

You can get through this stage without the help of a real estate agent or lawyer. The rest must be handled by any reputable Dubai real estate agency or law firm.

2. SIGNING THE SALES CONTRACT

Signing a Sale and Purchase Agreement, also referred to as a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU), is the second legal step in buying property in Dubai.

Memorandum of Understanding or Form F is one of RERA's forms for real estate in Dubai. Form/Contract F is available on the official website of the Dubai Land Department. Usually a real estate agent prepares a contract for you.

Once the contract is ready, both the buyer and the seller must sign it in front of a witness (usually an agent) at the Registrar's Trustee's office.

The buyer must also pay a standard 10% security deposit to the Registration Trustee, which is returned upon completion of the transfer of ownership.

3. APPLICATION FOR A NON-OBJECTION CERTIFICATE

Your next step is to meet with the developer, seller and real estate agent.

The purpose of this meeting is to apply for and pay for a No Objection Certificate (NOC) to transfer ownership. The developer will only issue a NOC if the property does not have an outstanding maintenance fee.

4. CARRYING OUT THE TRANSFER OF OWNERSHIP WITH DLD

Once you have received your NOC, the final legal step when buying property in Dubai is to meet with the seller at the Dubai Land Department office for the transfer to take effect.

In order for the transfer of property to take effect, you must have the following documents with you before visiting the DLD office:

Manager's check for the property price payable to the seller

Original identity documents of the buyer and seller (Emirates ID, passport and visa)

Original NOC issued by the developer

Signed contract F (MOU)

A new title deed will be issued in your name once the formalities are completed and you will become the official owner of the property in Dubai.
